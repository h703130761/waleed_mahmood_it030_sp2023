Red Velvet Cake Recipe

Cake is my favorite dessert. Here is a recipe for a tasty Red Velvet Cake.

Ingredients

  • cocoa powder
  • sugar
  • butter
  • milk
  • eggs
  • flour

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, and cocoa powder.
  3. In another large bowl, whisk together the milk, butter, and eggs.
  4. Using a standing mixer, mix the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients until just combined and a smooth batter is formed.
  5. Divide the cake batter evenly among the prepared cake pans. Place the pans in the oven evenly spaced apart. Bake, rotating the pans halfway through the cooking, until the cake pulls away from the side of the pans.
  6. Remove the cakes from the oven and run a knife around the edges to loosen them from the sides of the pans.

Read more about the Red Velvet Cake origins

The Velvet cake is thought to have originated in Maryland in the early 20th century. During this period, the devil's food cake was introduced, which is how some believe that red velvet cake came about. The key difference between the two cakes is that devil's food cake uses chocolate and red velvet cake uses cocoa. When foods were rationed in the US during World War II, bakers used boiled beet juices to enhance the color of their cakes. Beet was and is used in some recipes as a filler or to retain moisture. The cake and its original recipe are well known in the United States from New York City's famous Waldorf-Astoria Hotel, which has been dubbed the confection Waldorf-Astoria cake.
